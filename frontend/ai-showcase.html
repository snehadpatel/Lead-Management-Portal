Why Random Forest?

Human behavioral data (clicks, form fills) is inherently non-linear. RF isolates overlapping variables into thousands of independent Decision Trees, preventing overfitting.

Why NOT Logistic Regression?

Assumes linear relationships between variables. Would aggressively misclassify leads from our chaotic CRM dataset.

4 Investor Personas

🚀 Growth Seeker — High Risk
🛡️ Wealth Preserver — Low Risk
⚖️ Balanced Allocator — Medium
📊 Index Tracker — Passive

Why K-Means?

Unlabeled psychological survey data. K-Means defines k=4 geometrical boundaries via Euclidean similarity, mapping human behavior into pitchable personas.

Why NOT DBSCAN?

DBSCAN ejects outliers as "Noise." In finance, every investor must be routed — we can't classify anyone as noise.

Engine 3: PyTorch LSTM

NAV / Price Forecaster

DEEP LEARNING

Architecture

  • ✅ PyTorch LSTM neural network
  • ✅ Sequence window: 30 timesteps
  • ✅ NIFTY 50 historical data (3 years)
  • ✅ Min-Max normalization
  • ✅ 5-day forward projection

How LSTM Works

LSTM cells maintain internal states that "remember" previous market volatility while projecting future trends. The forget gate, input gate, and output gate control information flow.

Why LSTM?

Fundamentally understands temporal patterns in sequential financial data.

Why NOT ARIMA?

ARIMA expects stationary patterns. NIFTY spikes are wildly unpredictable — ARIMA would flatline while LSTM rides the volatility.

Engine 4: TF-IDF + NLP

Semantic Search & Sentiment Analysis

NLP

Architecture

  • ✅ TF-IDF vectorizer (20K features)
  • ✅ Bigram n-gram range (1,2)
  • ✅ Cosine similarity matching
  • ✅ 14,275 AMFI fund schemes
  • ✅ Logistic Regression sentiment

Dual Use

Search: Converts human queries to geometric vectors. Cosine matching finds funds in milliseconds.

Sentiment: Classifies financial text as positive/negative/neutral for market mood analysis.

Why TF-IDF?

Converts vocabulary to geometric matrices. Operating a 14K × N array with Cosine Match achieves millisecond latency.

Why NOT BERT?

BERT requires massive GPU compute. Processing 14,275 funds through a 500-layer transformer would crash the memory heap.
